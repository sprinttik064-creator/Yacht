# NIU — Competitive Scan: Coconut Water as a Mixer (July 2026)

> Feeds the "category is open" slide of the pitch deck. FX: £1 ≈ €1.17, $1 ≈ €0.90.

## Verdict

**The club/bottle-service channel for coconut water is open — with a closing window.**
Incumbents are health-positioned grocery brands; Vita Coco's bar activity is an
awareness vehicle using retail packaging; Jax Coco proved luxury on-trade demand a
decade ago but never scaled; no premium-mixer house (Fever-Tree, Franklin & Sons,
Three Cents) has a coconut SKU.

**Three proof points for the slide:**
1. **Unbranded demand is already at the table.** Encore Beach Club (Vegas) sells
   coconut water as a paid premium mixer add-on in bottle service — the serve
   exists, the brand slot is empty. (discotech.me/las-vegas/ebc-at-night/bottle-service)
2. **Bartender-validated 2026 trend.** VinePair's 30-bartender panel calls coconut
   water the flavor "on just about every cocktail menu in 2026"; Taylor Swift's
   Bacardi-and-coconut-water VMA moment mainstreamed the serve. (vinepair.com)
3. **The playbook is proven and unapplied.** Fever-Tree: ~55% on-trade revenue,
   11 straight years the #1 mixer, built on a 200 ml glass single-serve at a 2–3×
   premium — a format no coconut water brand currently offers to bars.

**Slide framing caveat:** claim "no brand owns the club channel," NOT "coconut
water isn't in clubs" — Vegas menus and Vita Coco's stated intent are documented.

## Brand-by-brand

### Vaïvaï (FR)
Premium French lifestyle brand, "100% pure, no added sugar"; cocktails only a
secondary usage idea. Tetra 330 ml / 1 L, ~€4.5/L at Monoprix/Carrefour. Grocery +
some foodservice wholesale (Delidrinks). **No club evidence.**

### 100 Coconuts (US)
Hydration/lifestyle with a partnership engine (Disney Moana 2 cans, Inter Miami CF
official coconut water). Slim cans 325/490 ml, ~$2–2.5/can (≈€6–7/L). Sports venues,
not nightclubs; cocktail angle is marketing stunts. **No club evidence.**

### Bervera (UK)
⚠️ Spelling: **Bervera** (UK coconut water). **Berczy** is an unrelated British hard
seltzer — do not confuse in the deck. Pure/health positioning. Notable: already in
**200 ml bottles** (24-pack £36 → ~€1.76/unit ≈ €8.8/L) — the Fever-Tree size, but
sold as hydration via DTC/Amazon/health retail. **No on-trade play.**

### Vita Coco — the main strategic threat
Mass hydration brand, but since 2023 an explicit push "into bars, restaurants and
clubs": Coco Blanco cocktail program (Surf Lodge, Montauk), Diageo JV RTD "Spiked
with Captain Morgan". Weakness = our opening: they state bar presence is an
**awareness play for retail**, there is no dedicated bar-serve SKU (they pour from
retail tetra) and no bottle-service program. Madonna/Rihanna-era celebrity heritage.

### ZICO / Innocent / Rebel Kitchen
All wellness/grocery: ZICO reborn as founder-led "ZICO Rising" (sustainability),
Innocent 1 L chilled carton, Rebel Kitchen raw 750 ml. **Zero bar/club activity.**

### Other entrants
- **Good Nut** (Tom Brady × Gopuff, June 2026): 11.8 oz can, $3.29, Gopuff-exclusive,
  anti-sports-drink positioning. Category heat (+115% YoY coconut water on Gopuff),
  not a bar play.
- **Once Upon A Coconut**: 330 ml can, $2.49, Daymond John + athletes, health/charity.
- **Jax Coco** (HK/London, 2012) — the historical precedent: 238 ml sake-style glass
  bottle, explicitly a "premium mixer" in London's elite cocktail scene (Claridge's,
  Maybourne minibars). Proved luxury on-trade demand — and that it can stall boutique.
- **CocoTaps** (Las Vegas): fresh-tapped whole coconuts in 20+ Vegas resorts (Wynn,
  Aria, Bellagio). Novelty hydration serve, not a branded bottle-service mixer.
- **Premium mixer houses:** Fever-Tree, Franklin & Sons, Three Cents — **no coconut
  SKU in any range** as of July 2026.

## Benchmark: Fever-Tree 200 ml playbook
- 200 ml glass = one double-spirit serve; "if ¾ of your drink is the mixer, mix with
  the best."
- UK trade ~£1.01/bottle (≈€5.9/L); US retail $2.25/bottle (≈€10/L) — 2–3× premium
  over standard mixers, above nearly every coconut water's per-liter price.
- ~55% on-trade / 45% off-trade split (2024); entered via spirit-brand endorsements,
  100+ high-end accounts by end of 2005; CGA #1 selling & top-trending mixer 11 years
  running.

## Risks / counter-examples (for internal honesty)
1. Vegas already sells coconut water in bottle service — unbranded (Encore Beach Club).
2. Vita Coco has declared clubs a target and has Diageo muscle.
3. CocoTaps holds Vegas resort real estate (fresh-coconut novelty serve).
4. Jax Coco precedent: premium-glass on-trade play can work — and stall.
5. Celebrity crowding: Brady (Good Nut), Daymond John (Once Upon A Coconut),
   Vita Coco legacy. **No DJ/nightlife-native brand exists — that's the NIU slot.**
6. Avoid RTD/liqueur confusion in the deck: Vita Coco Spiked (5% ABV), CoCo Vodka,
   Toast Vodka, Coco López/Malibu (cream/liqueur). NIU is a 0.0% mixer.

## Addendum (Jul 2026): category-leader benchmark & the 0.75 L bar format

- **Fever-Tree (LSE: FEVR):** FY2025 revenue £375.3m (brand £372.7m); market cap ~£900–930m (Jun 2026); 70+ countries; #1 mixer by value in UK on- AND off-trade (CGA); Molson Coors took 8.5% for £71m (Jan 2025) and runs US distribution. Formats: 200 ml glass (core), 500 ml, 150 ml cans — **no 750 ml**.
- **Thomas Henry (Berlin, 2010):** last published revenue €34–35m (2020, up from €5m in 2014), ~80 employees, 50–60 countries, German on-trade premium-mixer leader; current revenue est. €40–50m [est.]. Formats: **0.2 L returnable glass + 0.75 L glass (expanded line)**, 0.75 L PET, 1.0 L PET; 750 ml glass leads its 2025 US launch.
- **0.75 L precedent:** Schweppes Germany added a 0.75 L returnable glass gastronomy bottle in 2019. Three Cents: 200 ml only. Franklin & Sons and London Essence top out at 500 ml. → 0.75 L bar bottle = continental gastronomy convention (TH + Schweppes DE), differentiator vs UK-style players.
